### 1d. Cross-page audit findings

1. **Hedged proof.** Many "metrics" are adjectives (`Higher`, `Faster`, `Cleaner`). The skill's landing-page checklist demands benefit-driven bullets — replace at least one per page with a defensible number or remove the metric tile.
2. **Testimonial monotony.** Every quote is anonymous and identically structured. Drafting plan adds a variant pattern (named role + region + measurable outcome) where we have permission, and a clearly-marked "illustrative" tag where we don't.
3. **Pain-point recycling.** "Silent participants" appears in Events, HR, Team Meetings. We'll keep the cross-cut but rotate the *consequence* (lost sponsor signal vs. attrition risk vs. decision rework).
4. **CTA homogeneity.** All bottom CTAs say the same two things ("Start free" / "View pricing"). Plan introduces page-specific secondary CTAs (e.g. Privacy → "Read our DPA"; Nonprofit → "Talk to governance team"; Consulting → "Book facilitator demo").
5. **SEO titles.** Most are within 60 chars; `useCases.teamMeetings.seo.title` is 56 chars and OK, but several descriptions exceed the soft 155-char target. Plan tightens all descriptions to ≤155 chars.
6. **AI claims (hard rule from `CLAUDE.md`).** No page should imply third-party model use. Plan replaces ambiguous "AI" claims with "Workers AI on Cloudflare's edge" wording on the AI Insights and Privacy pages.

## 2. Brand voice baseline (apply to every page)

Pulled from `CLAUDE.md` "WHY" and the existing Privacy page (the cleanest brand expression today):

- **Voice:** confident, evidence-first, never breathless. Sentences earn their length.
- **Vocabulary to favour:** "decision evidence", "consent log", "anonymity mode", "edge inference", "facilitator-first", "session-level", "audit-ready".
- **Vocabulary to avoid:** "revolutionary", "best-in-class", "AI-powered" as a standalone claim, "seamless", "leverage", em-dash sentence breaks for emphasis.
- **Numbers rule:** every number must be defensible. If we can't defend it, drop the tile or label it `Illustrative target`.
- **CTA voice:** verbs first ("Launch your next session", "Review the consent model"). No exclamation points.

This baseline plus `marketing:brand-review` becomes the post-draft gate.

## 3. Per-page drafting briefs

Each brief is sized to feed directly into one `/marketing:draft-content` invocation. Deliverable per page = a JSON patch for `solutions.json` covering only the keys for that page namespace.

### Wave 1 — Features (foundational, drafted first because Solutions/Use-Cases link back to them)

**F1 · AI Insights (`features.aiInsights.*`)**
- Content type: Landing page copy
- Audience: HR ops leaders + facilitation consultants evaluating whether Qesto's AI claim survives a privacy review.
- Key messages: (1) draft a question set in <90s, (2) themes appear without sending data to third-party LLMs, (3) editable by humans before and after — AI is a draft partner, not a decision-maker.
- Tone: confident, technical-credible, slightly dry.
- Length: hero subheadline ≤ 28 words; each step ≤ 22 words; metric notes ≤ 18 words.
- SEO: primary keyword `AI question generation`; secondary `theme detection survey`; meta description tightened to 152 chars.
- Risk to handle: "AI" without source provenance — must reference Cloudflare Workers AI explicitly in step 1 or step 2.

**F2 · Live Polling (`features.livePolling.*`)**
- Content type: Landing page copy
- Audience: event producers, internal-comms leads who already use Mentimeter/Slido and need a switching reason.
- Key messages: (1) one session, every interaction format (poll / ranking / consent / open) — no tool stitching, (2) global edge keeps latency consistent for hybrid rooms, (3) host pacing controls feel familiar to current Mentimeter users.
- Tone: practical, comparative without naming competitors.
- Length: same constraints as F1.
- SEO: primary `live polling tool`; secondary `ranked Q&A`, `interactive presentation polls`.
- Risk: don't repeat AI claims here — keep this page about real-time mechanics.

**F3 · Privacy (`features.privacy.*`)**
- Content type: Landing page copy (anchor page for brand voice)
- Audience: security/compliance buyers (CISO, DPO) reviewing for GDPR/SOC-style procurement.
- Key messages: (1) edge AI = data does not leave Cloudflare boundary, (2) per-session anonymity modes are a configuration not a setting hidden behind enterprise tier, (3) consent log is exportable and time-stamped.
- Tone: precise, sober, no hype.
- Length: subheadline ≤ 30 words; outcome notes ≤ 20 words.
- SEO: primary `GDPR consent voting`; secondary `anonymous workplace surveys`, `edge AI privacy`.
- Risk: avoid promising specific certifications (SOC2, ISO27001) unless verified — use "audit-ready", "designed for".

### Wave 2 — Solutions (verticals; reuse Wave-1 features in `related` blocks)

**S1 · Consulting (`consulting.*`)**
- Audience: principal/senior consultants and partners running paid client workshops.
- Key messages: (1) replace sticky-notes + manual synthesis, (2) leave clients with same-day decision evidence, not photos, (3) reusable templates across engagements.
- Tone: peer-to-peer (consultant-to-consultant), confident.
- Length & SEO: meta description tightened; primary `workshop facilitation tool`; secondary `client workshop deliverables`.
- Differentiator vs. U2 (Workshops): Consulting page sells the *commercial model* (billable evidence, recap velocity); U2 sells the *facilitation craft*.

**S2 · Events (`events.*`)**
- Audience: programme directors, event producers, sponsorship leads at conferences 200–10k attendees.
- Key messages: (1) sponsor-grade engagement evidence beyond impressions, (2) one join code from keynote through breakout, (3) speaker-side adjustments mid-session.
- Tone: punchier than the rest; events buyers respond to immediacy.
- SEO: primary `live audience polling`; secondary `conference engagement platform`, `ranked Q&A`.
- Net new: sponsor-recap angle in pain points + a scenario for sponsored sessions.

**S3 · HR & People (`hr.*`)**
- Audience: People/CHRO function at 200–2000-person companies running pulse programs.
- Key messages: (1) anonymity *modes* (full / cohort / identified) per session, (2) high-frequency cadence without survey fatigue, (3) AI themes are edge-processed — no employee text leaves Cloudflare.
- Tone: trust-forward, careful with promises.
- SEO: primary `anonymous pulse survey`; secondary `employee feedback platform`, `GDPR HR survey tool`.
- Net new: explicit anonymity-mode list in the features grid.

**S4 · Nonprofits (`nonprofit.*`)**
- Audience: board secretaries, member-org operations leads, governance committees.
- Key messages: (1) consent voting (not just yes/no), (2) inclusive remote participation by short code, (3) audit trail suitable for AGM minutes.
- Tone: civic, careful, low-jargon.
- SEO: primary `online consent voting`; secondary `nonprofit board voting`, `AGM voting platform`.
- Risk: soften "tamper-evident" — replace with "verifiable", "auditable" unless we ship cryptographic proofs.

### Wave 3 — Use cases (horizontals; orthogonal to verticals)

**U1 · Team Meetings (`useCases.teamMeetings.*`)**
- Audience: engineering managers, product leads, distributed-team operators.
- Key messages: (1) consent rounds before closing decisions, (2) recap exports into Slack/Notion/Linear, (3) decision rationale stays linked to the session.
- Tone: practitioner, slightly informal.
- SEO: primary `team meeting decision tool`; secondary `retrospective polling`, `sprint planning prioritization`.
- Net new: name target export surfaces (Slack, Notion) in features.

**U2 · Workshops (`useCases.workshops.*`)**
- Audience: in-house facilitators, design-thinking practitioners, L&D facilitators.
- Key messages: (1) hybrid parity for remote participants, (2) AI clustering replaces manual affinity sorting, (3) shareable structured outputs.
- Tone: craft-focused (facilitator's craft), not commercial.
- SEO: primary `hybrid workshop tool`; secondary `affinity clustering AI`, `design-thinking facilitation`.
- Differentiator vs. S1: facilitator practitioner voice, not commercial-deliverable voice.

**U3 · Training (`useCases.training.*`)**
- Audience: L&D managers, instructional designers, enablement leads.
- Key messages: (1) formative checks at module boundaries, (2) confidence scales catch quiet learners, (3) reflection prompts feed retros and L&D reporting.
- Tone: pedagogically literate (reference Kirkpatrick L1/L2 in subhead or scenario).
- SEO: primary `formative assessment tool`; secondary `live training polls`, `learner confidence survey`.
- Net new: one scenario aligned to onboarding cohorts (high-volume L&D use case).

## 5. Quality gates (run after each page draft)

1. `/marketing:brand-review` against the §2 voice card — flag any banned vocabulary.
2. SEO check — title ≤ 60 chars, description ≤ 155 chars, primary keyword in headline + first paragraph.
3. **Hard-rule check from `CLAUDE.md`:** no third-party AI references; AI mentions tied to Workers AI / edge.
4. JSON validity — `node -e "JSON.parse(require('fs').readFileSync('public/locales/en/solutions.json','utf8'))"`.
5. FAQ schema lint — verify FAQ JSON-LD blocks render (3 Q&A per page minimum, ≤ 80-word answers).
6. Visual smoke — render each page locally, check that rewritten copy doesn't break the template grid (long headlines wrapping into hero image).
7. Trigger `/i18n` to regenerate `nl/de/fr/es` only after EN copy is approved.
