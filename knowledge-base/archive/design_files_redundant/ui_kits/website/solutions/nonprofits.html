How a motion moves

Four steps from "any objections?" to signed minutes.

Step 01

Chair calls the vote

Pick the motion from the agenda, set identified or secret, and launch. Every director's device lights up.

Step 02

Directors cast

For, against, abstain. Quorum tracked live. Proxy votes pre-loaded against the director's name.

Step 03

Tally locked

Chair seals the vote. Tally freezes on the projected screen. Changes require re-opening with a new motion.

Step 04

Minutes exported

Signed PDF with every motion, tally, and consent log. Sent to the secretary before adjournment.

What you get on every meeting

A governance tool, not a polling gimmick.

Quorum tracking. Live count of present vs. required, surfaced to the chair.
Proxy support. Pre-load proxy votes against absent directors' names, with delegate on record.
Roll-call mode. Each vote logged by name, in order, for bylaw compliance.
Secret ballot mode. Tally without identities — useful for officer elections and sensitive motions.
Conflict disclosure. Directors flag conflicts per motion; noted in minutes automatically.
Consent log. Every director's visibility choice stamped to the meeting — auditable by your secretary.
Signed PDF minutes. Export signed with a per-session hash; any edit invalidates.
Bylaw templates. Starter templates for 501(c)(3), UK CIO, Stichting, Verein structures.
