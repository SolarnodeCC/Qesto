# Enterprise Data Controls

For organizations with compliance, data-sovereignty, or multi-org requirements, Qesto offers
controls beyond the standard plans. These are **Chorus / enterprise** capabilities and are
typically configured by a team owner or administrator. For the full legal and security detail,
your security team can also refer to Qesto's compliance documentation; this page is the
plain-language overview.

## Data Residency

Pin a team's data to a specific region so that session data stays where your policy requires.

- An owner sets the team's **residency region**.
- Once pinned, Qesto enforces that the team's data is stored and processed in that region.
- Useful for meeting EU (or other regional) data-handling requirements.

## Federation (cross-org trust)

Federation lets separate Qesto organizations form **trust links** so they can collaborate —
for example, sharing a session library or running joint sessions — without merging into one
tenant. Federation is **consent-based**: both organizations must opt in to the link, and each
side keeps control of its own data and can revoke the link.

## Sovereign+ Tier

The **Sovereign+** tier is for the strictest data-sovereignty needs. On top of residency it
adds:

- **Per-region edge residency** — data stays within a designated sovereign boundary.
- **Compliance posture matrix** — a view of how the tenant maps to its compliance controls.
- **Verifiable audit export** — a signed export of audit activity that an auditor can
  independently verify hasn't been tampered with.
- **Federation eligibility controls** — decide whether (and with whom) a sovereign tenant may
  federate.
