# Semantic Decision Search

Qesto can surface past sessions and questions that are *related in meaning* to what
you're writing now — even if they don't share any exact keywords. This is semantic
decision search.

## Where you'll see it

While authoring questions in **Studio**:

1. Start typing a question
2. Qesto embeds your draft text and compares it against your team's past sessions
3. Related historical questions/sessions appear as suggestions for your next question

This works on meaning, not string matching — a question about "burnout" can surface
a past session about "team exhaustion" even though the words don't overlap.

## What it searches

- Only sessions belonging to **your team** — suggestions never cross into another
  team's or tenant's data
- Past session titles and questions, not raw participant responses

## Plans

- **Pulse** — not included
- **Signal** and **Chorus** — included

## Tips for better matches

- Write a full sentence, not a single word — more context gives a better match
- If no related sessions exist yet, you'll simply see no suggestions (this is
  expected for new teams)

## Questions?

**Q: Does semantic search read participant answers?**
A: No — it compares your draft question text against past session/question
content, not individual participant responses.

**Q: Why didn't I get any suggestions?**
A: Either your team has no sufficiently similar past sessions yet, or the match
confidence was too low to show. Try writing a more complete question.

**Q: Is this the same as AI Insights?**
A: No. AI Insights (Chorus) extracts themes from closed-session responses.
Semantic decision search helps you author new questions by finding related past
ones — it's available starting on the Signal plan.
